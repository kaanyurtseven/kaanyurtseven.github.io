Electricity grids are becoming harder to operate as renewable energy grows and uncertainty becomes part of everyday decision making.

I work on optimization tools that turn that uncertainty into something decision makers can actually use. The goal is not only to model complexity, but to make better choices possible in systems where reliability, economics, and risk all matter at the same time.

---

## What drives my work

Three ideas sit at the center of what I do:

### Risk-aware decision making for power system operation and planning

Good decisions are not only about expected cost. They also depend on how uncertainty can shift outcomes, create exposure, and change the value of operational actions.

This part of my work focuses on decision tools that account for risk explicitly, so that planning and operation can be guided not only by cost efficiency, but also by how uncertainty may actually play out.

---

### Optimization under uncertainty

Many optimization models still rely on simplified deterministic assumptions, even though real electricity systems do not behave that way.

Here the focus is on building optimization frameworks that represent uncertainty directly and show how it affects technical and economic outcomes. That includes stochastic formulations, uncertainty propagation, and methods that preserve the structure of the underlying power system problem.

---

### Renewable integration in future electricity grids

The energy transition is not only about adding more renewables. It is also about operating electricity systems in a way that can absorb variability, respond to uncertainty, and still support reliable decisions.

This part of the work looks at how optimization and uncertainty modeling can support the integration of renewable energy in increasingly complex grid environments.

---

## What this looks like in practice

Across these themes, the work often leads to tools and frameworks for questions such as:

- how to make operational decisions when uncertainty cannot be ignored
- how to quantify the economic impact of risk
- how to manage congestion in renewable-dominated systems
- how to use flexibility in hybrid AC/DC grids more effectively
- how to translate uncertainty into information that supports action

---

## Core building blocks

The methods behind this work include:

- stochastic optimization
- risk-aware optimization
- power system modeling
- non-Gaussian uncertainty representation
- hybrid AC/DC grid analysis
- decision-support tools for complex electricity systems
