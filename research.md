# Research

Modern electricity systems are undergoing a profound transformation. The rapid growth of renewable energy introduces significant uncertainty into generation and demand forecasts, making planning and operational decisions increasingly complex. At the same time, electricity grids must continue to operate reliably and efficiently while enabling the energy transition.

My research focuses on developing optimization tools that transform uncertainty into actionable information for decision making in electricity systems. By combining power system modeling with advanced optimization and uncertainty representation techniques, I study how complex electricity systems can operate and evolve under increasing uncertainty.

---

## Research Themes

### Risk-aware decision making for power system operation and planning

Operational and planning decisions in electricity systems increasingly involve risk. Forecast uncertainty, renewable variability, and market conditions all influence the outcomes of operational actions such as redispatch, congestion management, and balancing.

My research develops optimization frameworks that explicitly account for risk when making operational and planning decisions. Instead of treating uncertainty as a secondary consideration, these methods incorporate uncertainty directly into the decision process, allowing decision makers to evaluate trade-offs between cost, reliability, and risk.

These approaches support more informed decisions in complex electricity systems where uncertainty plays an important role.

---

### Optimization under uncertainty

Many traditional power system optimization models assume deterministic inputs. However, modern electricity systems operate under substantial uncertainty due to renewable generation, demand variability, and market dynamics.

My work focuses on optimization frameworks that explicitly represent uncertainty and quantify its impact on system behavior. This includes the development of stochastic optimization models and uncertainty propagation techniques that allow decision makers to evaluate how uncertain inputs influence operational outcomes.

By incorporating uncertainty directly into optimization models, these methods provide a more realistic basis for decision making in modern electricity systems.

---

### Renewable integration in future electricity grids

The energy transition requires electricity systems to integrate large shares of renewable energy while maintaining reliability and efficiency. Achieving this goal requires new operational and planning approaches capable of managing increased uncertainty and variability.

My research studies how electricity grids can accommodate high renewable penetration through improved operational strategies and decision-support tools. This includes analyzing how flexibility resources, network structures, and operational decisions interact with uncertainty in renewable-dominated systems.

The goal is to enable electricity systems that can reliably integrate renewable energy while supporting efficient and informed decision making.

---

## Methodological Focus

My research combines several methodological areas:

- **Stochastic optimization** for decision making under uncertainty  
- **Risk-aware optimization frameworks** that quantify operational risk  
- **Power system modeling** for AC and hybrid AC/DC grids  
- **Uncertainty representation techniques** for non-Gaussian uncertainties  
- **Decision-support tools** that translate complex uncertainty into actionable insights

These methods allow complex electricity system behavior to be analyzed while explicitly accounting for uncertainty and risk.

---

## Connection to the Energy Transition

Electricity systems are central to the energy transition. As renewable penetration increases, uncertainty becomes a fundamental aspect of system operation and planning.

By developing optimization tools that transform uncertainty into structured information for decision making, my research contributes to enabling electricity systems that are both reliable and capable of integrating large shares of renewable energy.
